Enhancing Textual Steganography with Large Language Models

This systematic literature review examines the transformative impact of Large Language Models (LLMs) on linguistic steganography. Our analysis shows LLM-based approaches significantly enhance imperceptibility, embedding capacity, and naturalness, addressing key limitations of traditional methods. This interactive report explores the findings, challenges, and future of this rapidly evolving field.

Future Research Directions

Based on our analysis, we identify several promising and critical directions for future research and innovation in this domain.

Balancing Security & Quality

Developing techniques that better balance perceptual quality (human undetectability) and statistical security (machine undetectability).

Increasing Embedding Capacity

Creating methods to increase the amount of hidden data without compromising the imperceptibility or fluency of the cover text.

Improving Contextual Control

Designing approaches for finer-grained semantic control and stronger contextual consistency in the generated text.

Strengthening Security Guarantees

Advancing the theoretical foundations of LLM-based steganography to provide stronger, provable security guarantees against advanced adversaries.

Ethical Frameworks

Establishing robust ethical frameworks and safeguards for the responsible development and deployment of these powerful technologies.

Practical Deployment Models

Investigating methods that are effective in real-world, black-box scenarios where only API access to LLMs is available.
